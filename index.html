Baby Sign Language

Babies are smarter than you think. Babies can start to understand language as early as three-months, even though most children don’t start speaking until about a year old. Baby sign language can help parents communicate with their child before the child develops vocal language skills, here are some signs to get you started.

More

More

To sign more, pinch your thumb and other four fingers together on each hand then bring your hands together, and pull them apart horizontally.

Please

To sign Please, open your hand and turn it so that your palm is facing you. Then rub it in a circle on your chest.

Food

Food

To sign food, pinch your thumb and other four fingers together on one hand and then raise that hand to your lips. Move your hand back and forth a few inches, to and from your mouth to illustrate the sign.

Diaper

To sign diaper, move your hand to your waist and touch your index and middle fingers to your thumbs on each hand.

Milk

To sign milk, bring your hand to chest height and open and close your fist.

Help

Help

To sign help, open your palm and place your other hand on top of your open palm with your thumb sticking up. Then move both of your hands together in a motion that’s similar to lifting up.

Bath

Bath

To sign bath, make fists with both your hands and move them vertically up and down in front of your chest, in a scrubbing motion.

Done

Done

To sign done, or all done, raise both of your open palms facing you to your shoulders and then flip them outwards.

Water

Water

To sign water, take one hand and use your thumb to hold down your pinky, and extend your three fingers. Then tap your index finger to your chin.

Cat

Cat

To sign cat, bring your hand up to your cheek and pinch your index and thumb together, then pull your hand a few inches away from your cheek, mimicking whiskers.
